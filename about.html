Sample language describing why AI impact is measured and how AI may affect work.

Why measure AI impact?

AI impact is measured to help workers, educators, employers, and policymakers better understand how technology may change the tasks performed within occupations. The goal is not to assume that AI will replace entire jobs, but to identify where work is most likely to be augmented, redesigned, or reorganized. Measuring exposure can help states and institutions prioritize career guidance, reskilling strategies, and program alignment so that people can move toward occupations with stronger long-term demand and lower disruption risk.

AI may affect jobs in different ways depending on the occupation. In some roles, AI can automate repetitive administrative or information-processing tasks, allowing workers to focus on higher-value activities. In other roles, AI may increase productivity without reducing the need for workers, especially where human judgment, interpersonal interaction, compliance, safety, or hands-on service remain essential. Because these effects vary across occupations and industries, a transition pathways tool can support more proactive planning by highlighting related careers, labor market conditions, and training opportunities that may help workers adapt over time.
